Food Pyramid Diary

The food pyramid below encorporates six levels which range from healthier foods from the bottom of the pyramid to less healthier foods at the top of the pyramid. The visual representation of the pyramid coincides with the functionality of the pyramid, as the food at the bottom of the pyramid is to be eaten more than the food at the top. There is also additional information surrounding intake and portion sizes.The recommended daily calorie intake is 2,000 calories a day for women and 2,500 for men.

  • level 1 Food Group: Foods and Drinks high in Fat, Sugar & Salt.
  • level 2 Food Group: Fats,Spreads & Sweets.
  • level 3 Food Group: Meats, Poultry, Fish, Eggs, Beans & Nuts.
  • level 4 Food Group: Milk, Yoghurts & Cheese.
  • level 5 Food Group: Wholemeal Cereals, Potatoes, Pasta, Rice & Breads.
  • level 6 Food Group: Vegetables, Salad, Fruit.
  • Recommended intake of level 1: Not Every Day.(maximum once or twice a week)
  • Recommended intake of level 2: In Very Small Amounts.
  • Recommended intake of level 3: 2 Servings a day.
  • Recommended intake of level 4: 3 Servings a day.
  • Recommended intake of level 5: 3-5 Servings a day.
  • Recommended intake of level 6: 5-7 Servings a day.
Foods that contain the same type of nutrients are grouped together on each of the shelves of the Food Pyramid. This gives you a choice of different foods from which to choose a healthy diet. Following the Food Pyramid as a guide will help you get the right balance of nutritious foods within your calorie range. Studies show that we take in too many calories from foods and drinks high in fat, sugar and salt, on the top shelf of the Food Pyramid. They provide very little of the essential vitamins and minerals your body needs. Limiting these is essential for healthy eating.
